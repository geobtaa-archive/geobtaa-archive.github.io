## Project Task Force Members

Nathan Piekielek, Geospatial Services Librarian; Paige Andrew, Maps Cataloging Librarian and Linda Ballinger, Metadata Strategist<br/><br/>Over the course of the past three years Penn State University Libraries has organized and shared through the BTAA geoportal cartographic information resources of two primary types: 1) digital surrogates of original paper maps and atlases; and 2) geospatial datasets. Paper maps and atlases are from The Donald W. Hamer Maps Collection and Eberly Family Special Collections Library. Geospatial data are from state agencies across Pennsylvania that have been contributed to The Pennsylvania Geospatial Data Clearinghouse ([PASDA](http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.pasda.psu.edu%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw2GpSD3VT9d_lVWjKdKPyfp)). Some examples of each follow:<br/>A nearly complete collection of Pennsylvania Sanborn Fire Insurance Maps that [when properly georeferenced](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fsites.psu.edu%2Fmapsgislib%2Faccuracy-assessment-of-sanborn-fire-insurance-maps%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw2eKtssoj00rPlCgtlB5k8y) can be used to [track land use change through time](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fsites.psu.edu%2Fmapsgislib%2Fabout-projects%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw1gqyuN3tyChfjCXW_RO--3) and can be linked to other historical human geography data sources like [manuscript censuses and university student directories](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fsites.psu.edu%2Fmapsgislib%2Fabout-sanborn-mapping-people%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw1W6bbTb3ecyU2tjOp-ircQ) to visually depict (at the building level) the locations of historical peoples and businesses within communities.<br/><br/><br/>
