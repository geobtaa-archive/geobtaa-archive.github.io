#### What are the items?



The BTAA Geoportal collates spatial layers from local and state agencies that cover a range of themes, including infrastructure. Among these layers are bridge datasets at the city, county, and state levels.



Browse the [bridges of BTAA Counties](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2F%3Ff%255Bgbl_resourceClass_sm%255D%255B%255D%3DDatasets%26q%3Dbridges%26search_field%3Dall_fields&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHZcHFtWpZ0qxT7KAyOaIJ-FQ0K4Q)





#### What BTAA Library submitted the item?



All [13 members](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fsites.google.com%2Fumn.edu%2Fbtaa-gdp%2Fabout&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEGVB-J-EpYWu1XXRcJcHkzjC1L2g) of the BTAA Geoportal have submitted datasets of bridges in their states.





Interesting tidbits:




- The types of data on bridges differ among states:
  - Some datasets, like [Nebraska Bridges](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F085238d8679a4e85a7e4ea08d3d40417_3&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHrMaqNeSqsQz3N5jWCu1tMQKYvEA), include the date of bridge construction and reconstruction for all bridges in the state. The oldest listed bridge in the dataset was constructed in 1888.
  - IowaDOT provides individual feature layers for bridges [proposed](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.waukonstandard.com%2Farticles%2F2019%2F07%2F17%2Fblack-hawk-bridge-lansing-be-replaced-july-9-meeting-shows-location-design&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNGQeOdLROBuidexxXHvoWmfkLrjNw) to replace the Black Hawk Bridge in Lansing, IA, including alternatives called the [Purple Bridge](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2Fb70501271bb94dc986bca8b3d2250c31_3&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEhxV15zTYp9iT-3Dw56-I1c73zfA), [Blue Bridge](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2Ff5d56a84a22643c3884ece6ac825d8cb_3&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFBAs1DIa_3i6H4yOqKWUTKz0d4cg), and [Green Bridge](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F25e5163a19104765b4006885cbb6ca9a_3&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFnc3Gfdbd_cBB10MeUAoMRsLtM2w).
  - [Maryland’s Historic Highway Bridges](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F10042f30213c4cb6844df16fa9fccce6_0&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFGXeEZWEHhWaaAvmBc_BbKHnBf7Q) contains historical bridges throughout the state. Images of the bridges can be viewed by clicking on a map pin in the geoportal and selecting the image URL in the table below the preview map.
- Many of the datasets can be explored through your browser. For example, data hosted in ArcGIS Online lets users sort and filter the data based on criteria like date of construction, location, type, last inspection date, and more.

Where can I find out more?


- The BTAA Geoportal contains data on historical and contemporary bridges, in addition to scanned maps of bridge locations.
- Users can use the Advance search function or the search facets to find bridge and infrastructure data for a given time period or location.
