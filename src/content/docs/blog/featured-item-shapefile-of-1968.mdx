#### What is the item?

In 1968, Ian McHarg created [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2F%3Futf8%3D%25E2%259C%2593%26search_field%3Dall_fields%26q%3DMcHarg&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHZ7URL_8amHcXY04SZWgbIFJm8EA](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2F%3Futf8%3D%25E2%259C%2593%26search_field%3Dall_fields%26q%3DMcHarg&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHZ7URL_8amHcXY04SZWgbIFJm8EA) [a series of maps showing the climate, geology, and environment of the Twin Cities in Minnesota](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2F%3Futf8%3D%25E2%259C%2593%26search_field%3Dall_fields%26q%3DMcHarg&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHZ7URL_8amHcXY04SZWgbIFJm8EA). These maps were commissioned by the Metropolitan Council, a first-of-its-kind regional governing body concerned about the impact of poorly managed urban development on natural spaces. The maps and report sought to determine the most suitable land uses for the region using an "ecology-first" lens. <br/><br/>A 6 x 6 foot, hand-drawn copy of McHarg’s Composite Intrinsic Suitability map is currently kept at the [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.lib.umn.edu%2Fborchert&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEf76ynkxXkzyHKBxtaBNZNSs76qA](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.lib.umn.edu%2Fborchert&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEf76ynkxXkzyHKBxtaBNZNSs76qA) [Borchert Map Library](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.lib.umn.edu%2Fborchert&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEf76ynkxXkzyHKBxtaBNZNSs76qA). Excitingly, it is now available as digitized vector data as well! <br/><br/>This new format makes it easier for researchers to examine land use change and the impacts of regional planning. When compared with [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2Fe26aeb17-7864-4317-a5ee-ecf0f9872b54&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNG3u9b56vU5KoFzp53VSvZL_YkO6w](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2Fe26aeb17-7864-4317-a5ee-ecf0f9872b54&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNG3u9b56vU5KoFzp53VSvZL_YkO6w) [data derived from 1968 land use maps](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2Fe26aeb17-7864-4317-a5ee-ecf0f9872b54&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNG3u9b56vU5KoFzp53VSvZL_YkO6w) or [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F7c8c7132-f03c-4b09-8c05-90b20b7db4b5&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFb-77jqbJjm2ZDuoyN-pzqolqF3A](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F7c8c7132-f03c-4b09-8c05-90b20b7db4b5&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFb-77jqbJjm2ZDuoyN-pzqolqF3A) [current parcels](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F7c8c7132-f03c-4b09-8c05-90b20b7db4b5&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFb-77jqbJjm2ZDuoyN-pzqolqF3A), it raises many possible questions:<br/>
- How closely did planners and developers follow the 1968 recommendations?
- Are there patterns to where recommendations were followed and where they were not?
- How do McHarg’s recommendations compare to more recent plans proposed for the region?
