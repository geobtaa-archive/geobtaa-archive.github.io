## Featured Item: [Shipwrecks (Great Lakes), Wisconsin 2014](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fgeo.btaa.org%2Fcatalog%2F35EC573E-282C-40DD-8512-CE14E033F989&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw1mSL6zhGxX7S8U-IXYLDxl)

<br/>This is a shapefile of 631 shipwrecks in the Great Lakes of Wisconsin. The Wisconsin Historical Society compiled the data.<br/><br/>

#### What BTAA Library submitted the item?

University of Wisconsin<br/><a href="https://geo.btaa.org/catalog/35EC573E-282C-40DD-8512-CE14E033F989" target="_blank"><img alt="Map of Shipwrecks (Great Lakes), Wisconsin 2014" src="https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/a/AVvXsEiqoOiyOcNtMntLX4t0qS8eqc99U7WqzyOwshLhmPEZMNEmebSPNAHPXJq24-yUvo6L_fiSyIvmi3QBkBVeK55cQyYrK7ufEgk9Ge8Gb8AeBaXZv7tk5lkzj6F9utEKxuerXaBYqaK5NpkM-HQy_RMeXRsnbyY9v6KdqFEoxq81LGE0icfCh1WglRXnkw=w558-h640" title="Map of Shipwrecks (Great Lakes), Wisconsin 2014"/></a><br/><br/>

### Interesting tidbits:


- There’s a good deal of interesting attribute data in the shapefile, including: the vessel type, where the vessel was built, the lives lost in the wreck, and more.
- Using this dataset, you could create a time animation using the wreck dates or a comparison of home ports and wreck locations.


### Where can I find out more?


- More information about Wisconsin shipwrecks, including a searchable database of the wrecks, is available here: [http://www.wisconsinshipwrecks.org/](http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.wisconsinshipwrecks.org%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw2oJXZ7RvwALscg7hnlYZqj)
- The Wisconsin Coastal Atlas ([http://www.wicoastalatlas.net/](http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.wicoastalatlas.net%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AOvVaw3_OORakFkDsvNoRnweAGKW)) is a great resource for maps, data, and tools for the region
