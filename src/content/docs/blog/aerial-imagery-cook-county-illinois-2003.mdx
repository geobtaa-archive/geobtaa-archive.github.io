#### What is the item?

This is black and white aerial imagery captured in 2003 for Cook County, Illinois at 6 inch pixel resolution. The imagery was taken with panchromatic film, which is film sensitive to all wavelengths of visible light and capable of high spatial resolution.<br/><br/>The Cook County imagery dating from 1998 is discoverable in the geoportal. Cook County Government makes the data available through the [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fhub-cookcountyil.opendata.arcgis.com%2Fdatasets%2Ff718375273e045f9a6679dcc6863d456&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNE-kDTMOvd1v0a6iRzPAbpfJYchCA](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fhub-cookcountyil.opendata.arcgis.com%2Fdatasets%2Ff718375273e045f9a6679dcc6863d456&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNE-kDTMOvd1v0a6iRzPAbpfJYchCA) [Cook Central Open Data Portal](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fhub-cookcountyil.opendata.arcgis.com%2Fdatasets%2Ff718375273e045f9a6679dcc6863d456&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNE-kDTMOvd1v0a6iRzPAbpfJYchCA) as a web service and as a downloadable data set.<br/><br/><br/>

#### Interesting tidbits:


- The 2000s in Chicago saw investments in green and public spaces and changes to zoning and land uses that are apparent when comparing the 2003 imagery to later years.
- A series of [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.chicagotribune.com%2Fcolumns%2Fblair-kamin%2Fct-biz-chicago-landscape-architecture-kamin-20191224-x5dcvt4vjnfpzm5uec5hf6pejq-story.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFp7qgoCoeL5LeHSCouVmHk_NGbCw](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.chicagotribune.com%2Fcolumns%2Fblair-kamin%2Fct-biz-chicago-landscape-architecture-kamin-20191224-x5dcvt4vjnfpzm5uec5hf6pejq-story.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFp7qgoCoeL5LeHSCouVmHk_NGbCw) [public spaces opened](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.chicagotribune.com%2Fcolumns%2Fblair-kamin%2Fct-biz-chicago-landscape-architecture-kamin-20191224-x5dcvt4vjnfpzm5uec5hf6pejq-story.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNFp7qgoCoeL5LeHSCouVmHk_NGbCw) in Chicago beginning in 2004. The 2003 imagery shows the construction of [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FMillennium_Park%23Reception_and_recognition&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHKLb9-XbUp5bDRWDEfuVQO15gyEg](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FMillennium_Park%23Reception_and_recognition&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHKLb9-XbUp5bDRWDEfuVQO15gyEg) [Millennium Park](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FMillennium_Park%23Reception_and_recognition&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHKLb9-XbUp5bDRWDEfuVQO15gyEg), a highly celebrated and controversial public space. It is also home to [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FCloud_Gate&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNF-MIYnDbPnVmb0IXZoJ1sXBVe26g](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FCloud_Gate&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNF-MIYnDbPnVmb0IXZoJ1sXBVe26g) [Cloud Gate](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fen.wikipedia.org%2Fwiki%2FCloud_Gate&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNF-MIYnDbPnVmb0IXZoJ1sXBVe26g), commonly referred to as the “Bean.”
- In 2004 Chicago approved a new zoning ordinance in response to a [http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.encyclopedia.chicagohistory.org%2Fpages%2F1401.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEtlYmfOmNONRaPxsjEHkZVyT7Deg](http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.encyclopedia.chicagohistory.org%2Fpages%2F1401.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEtlYmfOmNONRaPxsjEHkZVyT7Deg) [boom in residential construction](http://www.google.com/url?q=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.encyclopedia.chicagohistory.org%2Fpages%2F1401.html&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNEtlYmfOmNONRaPxsjEHkZVyT7Deg) that was, at least in part, to protect the aesthetics of Chicago neighborhoods. However, there is also a correlation between the [https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.stevencanplan.com%2F2016%2F05%2Foh-how-chicago-land-use-is-controlled-by-spot-zoning%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHhAo0ZTFcAPp6cGasaq7NBpxTOeg](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.stevencanplan.com%2F2016%2F05%2Foh-how-chicago-land-use-is-controlled-by-spot-zoning%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHhAo0ZTFcAPp6cGasaq7NBpxTOeg) [increase in discrete zoning districts](https://www.google.com/url?q=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.stevencanplan.com%2F2016%2F05%2Foh-how-chicago-land-use-is-controlled-by-spot-zoning%2F&amp;sa=D&amp;sntz=1&amp;usg=AFQjCNHhAo0ZTFcAPp6cGasaq7NBpxTOeg) since the ordinance passed. A comparison of the 2003 aerial imagery to later years will show the impact of the zoning measures.
