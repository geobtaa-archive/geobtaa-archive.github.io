The Big Ten Geospatial Information Network (BTAA-GIN) is a collaboration of library-affiliated staff from Big Ten universities. We support geospatial research, foster connections among library professionals, and advance open-source tools and practices for discovering and preserving spatial data.


  <section class="pillars" aria-labelledby="pillars-title">
  <h2 id="pillars-title" class="sr-only">Our focus areas</h2>

  <p class="pillars__lede">
    We focus on two key areas: <strong>technical infrastructure</strong> and <strong>social infrastructure</strong>, both of which support geospatial research and a collaborative community.
  </p>

  <div class="pillars__grid not-content">
    <article class="pillar">
      <header class="pillar__head">
        
        <h2 class="pillar__title">Technical infrastructure</h2>
      </header>
      <ul class="pillar__list">
        <li>

          <strong>BTAA Geoportal</strong> — improving discovery of maps, datasets, and services:
          <a href="https://geo.btaa.org"> geo.btaa.org</a>
        </li>
        <li>

          <strong>Metadata aggregation &amp; augmentation</strong> — normalizing, enriching, and optimizing records across platforms.
        </li>
        <li>

          <strong>Open-source contributions</strong> — active work in
          <a href="https://geoblacklight.org"> GeoBlacklight</a>,
          <a href="https://opengeometadata.org"> OpenGeoMetadata</a>, and
          <a href="https://geo4lib.org"> Geo4Lib</a>.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </article>

    <article class="pillar">
      <header class="pillar__head">

        <h2 class="pillar__title">Social infrastructure</h2>
      </header>
      <ul class="pillar__list">
        <li>

          <strong>Collaboration</strong> — opportunities to share knowledge and exchange best practices among geospatial library professionals.
        </li>
        <li>

          <strong>Annual community events</strong> — <a href="/conference/">conference + meetups</a> for students and practitioners.
        </li>
        <li>

          <strong>Guides &amp; tutorials</strong> — <a href="/user-resources/guides/">reference guides</a> and <a href="/user-resources/tutorials/">educational resources</a> for research and instruction.
        </li>
      </ul>
    </article>
  </div>
</section>
